    About this site #

    I’m nearing my 4 year anniversary at Benchling and, in the tradition of an undergraduate thesis at the end of college, I thought it’d be worth writing up all of my learnings in a place where others can read them.

    The target audience for this is current Benchlings and ex-Benchlings, though there’s nothing company-sensitive or non-public on this site.

    What is your goal in writing this? #

    My only goal is to write down what I’ve learned in a place where others can see it. I don’t expect to win any Pulitzer’s. I don’t expect my writing here to have any effect (positive or negative) on my day job on the Benchling product team.

    What inspired you to do this? #

    This site is partly inspired by Steve Yegge, Joel Spolsky, and Dan Luu. All three of them are far better writers than I am, so go read their blogs.

    I’ve also done a few talks, and it’s been fun to share learnings with newer folks in this industry (and see whether veterans agree or disagree with me). So this is in some ways a descendent of more traditional internal talks I give like “Life of an Experiment” and “What is a Study?” and external talks like “Machine Learning for R&D”.
